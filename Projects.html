Projects

From hair-thin jets and tattooing to the hydrodynamics of flamingo feeding, my projects span the intersection of physics, engineering, and biology. Each project below captures a unique exploration - whether it's building low-cost devices for drug delivery or decoding how animals move through and on water. Dive in to learn more about the science behind these stories...

Low-cost Electroporators

Inspired by BBQ lighters, low-cost pulser delivers mRNA and DNA to skin cells.

Science of splashes

How to make big splashes - revealing fluid-structure interactions in dynamic environments.

Water bending in Flamingoes

Flamingoes generate vortices by feet stomping, beak chattering, retraction and skimming.

Walking on water

Interfacial fluid dynamics of water walking in tiny insects and what it holds for microrobotics.

Fluid ejections in nature

Unifying organismal fluidic ejections from micro spores to elephants and whales.

Needle-free jet injection

Hair-thin jets deliver dyes and vaccines painlessly through the skin, mimicking tattooing.

Cavitation-induced microjets

Generating microjets using laser- and spark-induced cavitation for drug delivery.

Tattooing drugs

Tattooing penetrates the stratum corneum and creates channels for drug delivery to dermis.

Eye drug delivery

Beat the blink to effectively deliver the eyedrops to the eye, minimizing the drug wastage to the eye.

Injection mechanics

Counterpressure during injections in different parts of the skin and how to minimize it.
